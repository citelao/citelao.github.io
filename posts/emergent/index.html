Emergent behavior in "emergent"

Doctors and first responders use emergent differently than most people do. As in:

emergent (adj.) pertaining to an emergency Dorland’s Illustrated Medical Dictionary, via Emergency, Emergent, Urgent

For example: Our results suggest that urgent care clinics are successfully substituting for emergency departments in the treatment of non-emergent conditions (adapted from Lindsay et al, 2019).

But I’m more used to:

emergent (adj.) coming into existence Vocabulary.com

Or, more computer–science-y:

emergent (adj.) (philosophy, sciences) having properties as a whole that are more complex than the properties contributed by each of the components individually. Wiktionary

As in, As with most artificial life simulations, Boids is an example of emergent behavior[…] (Wikipedia Boids).

Laura King of AMA Style Insider attempts to resolve this in Emergency, Emergent, Urgent:

Resolution: Use emergent to mean emerging (as in Dorland’s section definition of “coming into being through consecutive stages of development, as in emergent evolution”) and emergency to mean an unexpected event that calls for immediate attention.
